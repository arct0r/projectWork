    Explanation for hazard conclusion

    Additional information - workers

    Due to its physico-chemical characteristics (inert substance, insoluble in water, does not cross biological membranes) it can be inferred that boron carbide is not systemically available, which is further supported by oral and dermal studies conducted with boron carbide (see chapters 7.2.1, 7.2.3 of the IUCLID dossier). In standard OECD tests 423 and 402 boron carbide exhibited no acute oral toxic characteristics and no acute dermal toxic characteristics when applied to rats at a concentration of 2,000 mg/kg body weight. In addition, all toxicological studies available for boron carbide showed no toxic characteristics of boron carbide indicating lack of uptake and absence of systemically availability. Therefore, the substance is unlikely to be taken up into the blood stream via the lungs. In addition no systemic or acute effects by all exposure routes have been observed by medical officers on workers at 4 high volume manufacturers of boron carbide (see statements attachment section 7.3). Boron carbide can be regarded as non-toxic and not systemically bioavailable.
