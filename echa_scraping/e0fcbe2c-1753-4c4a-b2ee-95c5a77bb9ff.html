    Workers - Hazard for the eyes

    Local effects

    Hazard assessment conclusion
    [Empty]
    Explanation for hazard conclusion
    [Not publishable]

    Additional information - workers

    In a study by Yeung et al (2005) rats received EDTA-FeNa at a level of 1200 mg Fe per kg diet for 39 days. Taking into account a consumption of ca. 25 g per day, and a mean weight of ca. 250 g during the study, rats received 30 mg Fe per day or 120 mg Fe per kg bw per day. This corresponds to: 421/56 x 120 =900 mg EDTA-FeNa.3H2O per kg bw/day. At this level no changes in growth rate were seen. If this study would be used to set a DNEL (But this has not been done due to lack of reporting other parameters than growth rate), the DNEL would be ca. 10 times higher.
