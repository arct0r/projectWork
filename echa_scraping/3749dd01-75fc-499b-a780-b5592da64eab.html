    Workers - Hazard for the eyes

    Local effects

    Hazard assessment conclusion
    no hazard identified
    Explanation for hazard conclusion
    [Not publishable]

    Additional information - workers

    The exposure limits for long-term local effects upon inhalation depend on the physical form of the substance. As the substance is not volatile, inhalation exposure is only possible to particles of an inhalble size. Particle deposition and clearance depend on particle size and solubility. Copper phthalocyanine particles with a measured MMAD of 0.5 - 1.1 micrometer caused no adverse effects in a short-term inhalation study (5 days plus three weeks recover) performed with 3, 10 and 30 mg/m3. From blue staininng by the pigment it was obvious that the pigment was both cleared via the gastrointestinal tract and via lung macrophages.

    The generic exposure limit of 10 mg/m3 is considered appropriate for particles of an inhalable size. However, for particles of an respirable size (alveolar fraction), lower threshols need to be applied and national occupational exposure limits need to be observed.
