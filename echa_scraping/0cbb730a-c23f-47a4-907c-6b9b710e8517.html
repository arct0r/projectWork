    Workers - Hazard for the eyes

    Local effects

    Hazard assessment conclusion
    high hazard (no threshold derived)
    Explanation for hazard conclusion
    [Not publishable]

    Additional information - workers

    Sodium aluminate is a strong base (pH > 11.5) and thus fulfils the criteria for classification as corrosive to the skin (category 1A) according to CLP regulation (1272/2008/EC). In addition, the substance also fulfills the requirements to be labelled as EUH071 corrosive to the respiratory tract. Thus, acute and/or repeated exposure via the dermal and/or inhalatory route would primarily result in local effects. Due to the corrosive properties of sodium aluminate, human exposure is unlikely to occur under normal working conditions as it should be avoided by implementation of the corresponding operational conditions and necessary risk management measures.
