    Additional information - workers

    Toxicological information “Matte, precious metal refining” and registration strategy

    Substance-specific information for the iUVCB substance “Matte, precious metal refining” (CAS 98072-52-7; EC 308-506-6) for acute and long-term toxicity is not available. In order to meet the criteria for registration under Regulation (EC) No 1907/2006 for substances > 1,000 t/y (Annex VII - X), read-across to identified, registered constituents is performed.

    The identified full reporting AE for “Matte, precious metal refining” is arsenic metal. Full data access (LtU) was granted to EPMF. The respective robust- and study summaries are included in IUCLID section 7. An extract of the human health hazard assessment (HH HA) is attached to IUCLID section 13.2 - Annex 3. A summary of the approach is provided below.

    Constituents considered for the identification of the full reporting constituent based on the composition profile are listed in the table below together with their hazard conclusions. The full reporting constituent is highlighted in red. Constituents were not considered if not classified as hazardous for human health and/or have a maximum concentration below the GCL/SCL.

    Calculations made with the classification tool MeClas are included in the individual HH endpoint study records based on the principles described under Regulation (EC) No 1272/2008, (also referred to as “mixture rules”) e.g.:

    - “acute toxicity range estimate (ATE)” and respective rules of Regulation (EC) No 1272/2006 section 3.1.3.6 “Classification of mixtures based on ingredients of the mixture” and

    This entire approach summarised above represents a conservative hazard assessment and accounts for the variability in the elemental composition of the iUVCB as detailed in the methodology paper attached to attached to IUCLID Section 13.2 Annex 7.

    Additional information - General Population

    DNELs for the general population are currently not included because an assessment of exposure of man via the environment is not reported but instead considered to be already included in the dossiers of the constituents. However, DNELs for the general population and the assessment of exposure of man via the environment will be amended by further analysis (please refer to Chapter 9.0.3. for further details).
