    Workers - Hazard for the eyes

    Local effects

    Hazard assessment conclusion
    no hazard identified
    Explanation for hazard conclusion 
    The substance has been tested for eye irritation and was found to be not subject to classification according to CLP (Regualtion (EU) No 1272/2008)

    Additional information - workers

    In the single available toxicity study, the test material was orally applied to Wistar rats for a total of 28 days at a dose level of 0 (control), 50, 200, 1000 mg/kg. The test was comprised of 4 groups, for a total of 60 rats. During the test periodno effects on mortality, clinical signs, food consumption, body weight, ophthalmoscopic examinations and organ weights.

    Treatment-related effects on hematology and clinical biochemistry were restricted to the high dose group (1000 mg/kg).Minor morphological alterations due to the administration of the test article were recorded in the stomach and kidneys.Based on the results of the study, 50 mg/kg test article was established as the no-observed-adverse-effect level (NOAEL) and as the no-observed-effect-level (NOEL).

    Based on the mentioned above, it was reasonable to choose the oral NOEL of 50 mg/kg bw/day as the starting point for the purpose of DNEL derivation.

    FAT40560 is expected to be absorbed in human via different routes - oral, dermal and inhalation route due to the physico-chemical properties (i.e., high molecular weight, highly water solubility, particle size is smaller than 50 Âµm, low Log Pow). Therefore, it is assumed that the absorption rate of oral is 30%. Moreover, the value of 20% skin absorption is chosen due to the molecular mass is above 1000 and log P is outside the range [-1, 4]. Therefore, NOAELcorr for the demal route is 75 mg/kg bw/day.

    According to ECHA guidance document the oral NOAEL is converted to an inhalatory NOAECWorker of 88.16 mg/m3. (0.38 m3/kg in case of 8 h exposure/d for worker), a factor of 0.67 for light work effects on respiratory volume and a factor of 30/30 was assumed in the case of oral-to-inhalation extrapolation (i.e. 30% for oral absorption, and 30% for inhalation).

    Treatment-related effects on hematology and clinical biochemistry were restricted to the high dose group (1000 mg/kg).Minor morphological alterations due to the administration of the test article were recorded in the stomach and kidneys.Based on the results of the study, 50 mg/kg test article was established as the no-observed-adverse-effect level (NOAEL) and as the no-observed-effect-level (NOEL).

    Based on the mentioned above, it was reasonable to choose the oral NOEL of 50 mg/kg bw/day as the starting point for the purpose of DNEL derivation.

    FAT40560 is expected to be absorbed in human via different routes - oral, dermal and inhalation route due to the physico-chemical properties (i.e., high molecular weight, highly water solubility, particle size is smaller than 50 Âµm, low Log Pow). Therefore, it is assumed that the absorption rate of oral is 30%. Moreover, the value of 20% skin absorption is chosen due to the molecular mass is above 1000 and log P is outside the range [-1, 4]. Therefore, NOAELcorr for the demal route is 75 mg/kg bw/day.

    According to ECHA guidance document the oral NOAEL is converted to an inhalatory NOAECgeneral population of 43.48 mg/m3. A factor of 1.15 m3/kg taking account of 24 h exposure/d for general population and a default factor of 30/30 was assumed in the case of oral-to-inhalation extrapolation (i.e. 30% for oral absorption, and 30% for inhalation).
