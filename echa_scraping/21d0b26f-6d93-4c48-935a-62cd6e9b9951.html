    Modified dose descriptor starting point
    NOAEC
    Value
    26,400 mg/mÂ³
    Explanation for the modification of the dose descriptor starting point

    The ECHA Committee for Risk Assessment (RAC) issued an opinion on methanol (ECHA/RAC/RESO-0000006324 -78 -01/F; ECHA, 2015). In this opinion, a DNEL for short-term oral exposures was derived based on the minimal oral methanol dose related to severe ocular toxicity; LOAEL = 260 mg/kg bw/day. An assessment factor of 3 was applied to this LOAEL to derive a oral short-term systemic DNEL of 88 mg/kg bw/day.

    The oral short-term systemic DNEL is used as the dose descriptor starting point for derivation of the inhalation short-term DNEL:

    The REACh standard daily inhalation volume for humans is 20 mÂ³/person/day.

    To convert this daily volume to a 15 minute volume: 20/(24 x 4) = 0.2 mÂ³/person/15 minutes.

    The DNEL of 88 mg/kg bw/day is equivalent to 5280 mg/person/day (assuming a body weight of 60 kg).

    Thus, to achieve a dose of 5280 mg/person during 15 minutes of exposure, the inhaled concentration would need to be 26400 mg/mÂ³.

    AF for dose response relationship
    1
    Justification

    The basis for the DNEL is a LOAEL, but the RAC DNEL already includes an assessment factor of 3.

    AF for interspecies differences (allometric scaling)
    1
    Justification

    LOAEL is based on human data

    AF for other interspecies differences
    1
    Justification

    LOAEL is based on human data

    AF for intraspecies differences
    1
    Justification

    RAC (ECHA/RAC/RES-O-0000006324-78-01/F) did not use an assessment factor for intraspecies differences

    AF for the quality of the whole database
    1
    Justification

    the data is based on good quality data

    AF for remaining uncertainties
    1
    Justification

    no remaining uncertainties
