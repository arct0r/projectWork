    Workers - Hazard for the eyes

    Local effects

    Hazard assessment conclusion
    high hazard (no threshold derived)
    Explanation for hazard conclusion
    [Not publishable]

    Additional information - workers

    Potassium methanolate is classified as corrosive to skin and eyes according to Annex VI of the CLP regulation (EC 1272/2008). Based on the skin and eye irritation data, it can be assumed that potassium methanolate will also cause irritation/corrosion to the mucous membranes of the upper respiratory tract in case of exposure via the inhalation route. At non-irritant concentrations/dose levels, no systemic effects are expected after repeated dose exposure to methanolates via any route as indicated by the available information on the hydrolysis/dissociation products. It is unlikely that exposure to potassium methanolate at non-irritant concentrations/dose levels would result in exposure to toxic doses of the hydrolysis/dissociation products, particularly methanol. Therefore, no DNEL was derived for potassium methanolate for systemic effects via inhalation or the dermal route of exposure. Based on the available data it is not possible to derive a DNEL for local effects, since local corrosion is the leading effect, which does not provide dose-response data. Therefore, a qualitative assessment as recommended by REACH Guidance documents (Part E (Risk Characterisation) and Chapter R.13 (Risk Management Measures and Operational Conditions)) is more appropriate and the DNELs of the hydrolysis products potassium hydroxide and methanol were not taken into account for the risk assessment of potassium methanolate.

    Additional information - General Population

    Since exposure of potassium methanolate for the general public is precluded, DNELs for the general population are not relevant and thus not derived. The DNELs of the hydrolysis products potassium hydroxide and methanol were also not taken into account as no risk assessment is necessary for the general population.
