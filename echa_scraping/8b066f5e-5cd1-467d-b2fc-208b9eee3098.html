    The calculation of the DNEL is based on an oral NOAEL observed in a repeated dose oral toxicity study (OECD 408; 1994).

    To correct the interspecies difference between rat and human the no observed effect level has to be corrected as follows:

    Corrected starting point for the inhalative route for workers:

    = NOAEL(oral) * (1/0.38 mÂ³/kg bw/day) * (ABSoral-rat/ABSinh-human) * 6.7 mÂ³ (8h) /10 mÂ³ (8h) * (days of exposure rat/days of exposure worker)

    = 110 mg/kg bw/day * (1/0.38 mÂ³/kg bw/day) * (1/2) * 0.67 mÂ³ * (7/5) = 135.8 mg/mÂ³

    As worst case as recommended in the ECHA Guidance R.8 (2012), it is assumed that oral absorption rate is 50% of that of of inhalation absorption.

    (ABSoral-rat = oral absorption in rats, ABSinh-human = inhalation absorption rate in humans)

    Thus, the corrected starting point for workers was 135.8 mg/mÂ³ for inhalation.

    Explanation for the modification of the dose descriptor starting point

    The calculation of the DNEL is based on an oral NOAEL observed in a repeated dose oral toxicity study (OECD 408; 1994).

    To correct the interspecies difference between rat and human the no observed effect level has to be corrected as follows:

    Dermal NOAEL = oral NOAEL*ABS(oral)/ABS(dermal) = 110 mg/kg bw/day *(100%/1%) = 11000 mg/kg bw/day.

    A dermal absorption rate of 1% is assumed as proposed in HERAG guidance for metals ( http://www.ebrc.de/downloads/HERAG_FS_01_August_07.pdf).
