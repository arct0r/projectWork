    Explanation for the modification of the dose descriptor starting point

    With respect to worker safety an internal occupational exposure limit (OEL) of 12 µg/m³ was derived for Norethisterone, based on the minimum therapeutic single human dose of 1.3 mg [= 1/4 tablet Primolut-Nor-5, according to internal documentation REGS-DE08-ATT-000600, Vs. 6 of 2019-10-14 and Patient leaflet (https://www.medicines.org.uk/emc/medicine/2547#gref, Feb 2022. Please refer to chapter 7.10.5.)]

    No substance specific OEL is available for Norandrostendione. Therefore, the OEL of Norethisterone of 12 μg/m³ is also adopted for Norandrostendione.

    The leading health effect after repeated exposure to steroidal progestins including progesterone among others is based on the interference with the body's hormonal balance. The toxic effects of steroidal estrogens are an exaggeration of the normal pharmacological effects and result in an increase of female characteristics.

    For such a systemic effect extrapolation from oral to inhalation is possible.

    SSTmin (dose descriptor starting point) divided by: (0.2* + 0.8** x f) x V = Modified dose descriptor starting point

    = 0,7 / ((0.2 + 0.8* 0.5) x 10) = 0.116666 mg/m3 = 116.66 µg/m3

     

    * proportion of the dose, which is absorbed via lung

    ** proportion of the dose, which is absorbed in the gastrointestinal tract

    f bioavailability after oral administration = 0.5 in the case of norethisterone

    V Volume of air inhaled during an 8-hours workshift = 10 m³

    Additional information - workers

    The minimum therapeutic single dose according to internal documentation (REGS-DE08-ATT-000600, Vs. 6 of 2019-10-14) is 1.3 mg Norethisterone (= 1/4 tablet Primolut-Nor-5). This is used as starting point for the derivation of the internal occupational exposure limit (OEL) of 12 µg/m³ for the inhalation route, or 0.7 mg/day for the dermal route.
