    Workers - Hazard for the eyes

    Local effects

    Hazard assessment conclusion
    medium hazard (no threshold derived)
    Explanation for hazard conclusion
    [Not publishable]

    Additional information - workers

    - No data are available that might indicate hazardous systemic or local effects after long-term or short-term inhalation. Therefore, no DNEL is derived for this route of exposure.

    - No data are available that might indicate hazardous systemic or local effects after long-term dermal exposure. Therefore, no long-term DNEL is derived for this route of exposure.

    - No data are available that might indicate hazardous systemic effects after short-term dermal exposure. Therefore, no DNEL is derived. The substance has been tested for skin irritation/corrosion and the evaluation of the results of the studies ('weight-of-evidence' approach) demonstrates that praseodymium trinitrate is not a dermal irritant/corrosive substance. As no adverse effect has been identified, no short-term DNEL for local effects is derived for this route of exposure.

    - Praseodymium trinitrate is classified as eye dam. 1 H318 according to the CLP Regulation (EC) 1272/2008. According to ECHA's Guidance on Information Requirements and Chemical Safety Assessment, Part E, Table E.3-1 the substance should be considered to cause medium hazard.
