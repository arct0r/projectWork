    Additional information - workers

    A DN(M)EL for acute effects should be derived if an acute hazard leading to acute toxicity (eg. C&L) has been identified and there is a potential for high peak exposures. This is not the case with 1,2 -butadiene.

    The long-term systemic DNEL of 371 mg/m3 is equivalent to 168 ppm.

    Additional information - General Population

    A DN(M)EL for acute effects should be derived if an acute hazard leading to acute toxicity (eg. C&L) has been identified and there is a potential for high peak exposures. This is not the case with 1,2 -butadiene. This chemical is an intermediate and therefore exposure of the general population is unlikely.

    The long-term systemic DNEL of 79 mg/m3 is equivalent to 36 ppm
