    Explanation for the modification of the dose descriptor starting point

    The calculation of the DNEL is based on a dermal NOAEL observed in a repeated dose toxicity study in rats (OECD 410). The frequency of treatment in the dermal repeated dose toxicity study was 5 days/week for the first three weeks and 7 days/week thereafter. As the animals were mainly exposed 5 days/week, the frequency of exposure is identical to the exposure frequency of the worker and no correction factor for the frequency was applied. Therefore, no modification of the dose descriptor starting point is neccessary.
