    Explanation for the modification of the dose descriptor starting point

    The NOAEL derived from an OECD 422 compliant oral repeated dose toxicity study of 200 mg/kg bw/d was applied as point of departure for derivation of long-term systemic inhalation DNEL. This NOAEL was corrected based on the following formula:

    Corrected NOAEC (inhalation) for workers:

    = 200 mg/kg bw/day x 0.5 x 1/0.38 m3/kg bw/day x (6.7 m3/10 m3) x 1.4

    = 246.8 mg/m3

    As worst case, inhalation absorption is assumed to be two times more than oral absorption which results in a modification of the starting point by a factor of 2.

    The factor of 1.4 corrects for exposure of 5 days per week for workers compared to 7 days per week in the experimental setup.

    Justification

    The exposure duration of the OECD TG 422 study performed with the test item up to 63 days for females and 29 days for males. In comparison to a subacute 28-day study the OECD TG 422 study provides additional information on fertility and developmental toxicity, which justifies the assessment factor of 4.
