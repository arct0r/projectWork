    Explanation for the modification of the dose descriptor starting point

    The default calculator has been applied: Starting point is NOAEL of 750 mg/kg bw/day from Combined repeated dose/reproduction screening in rat, where animals are dosed for 29-32 days (males) and average 54 days (females, range 50-67 days.

    The corrected 8 hr inhalation NOAEC for workers is calculated applying the default calculation: oral NOAELrat * 1/0.38 * 6.7/10. Additionally, a correction for differences between human and experimental exposure conditions of 1.4 is added (5 d/w versus experimental daily exposure). With the additional extrapolation factor of 2 from oral to inhalation route of exposure, this results to a NOAEC workers of 926 mg/m3.

    Explanation for the modification of the dose descriptor starting point

    The default calculator has been applied: Starting point is NOAEL of 750 mg/kg bw/day from Combined repeated dose/reproduction screening in rat, where animals are dosed for 29-32 days (males) and average 54 days (females, range 50-67 days. At this stage no data are available on dermal absorption and 100% dermal absorption is considered as worst-case assumption. Additionally a correction for differences between human and experimental exposure conditions of 1.4 is added (5 d/w versus experimental daily exposure): 8hr-NOAEL workers = NOAEL(750 mg/kg) * 1.4 = 1050 mg/kg bw/d.

Furthermore, DNELs for general public are not required as tonnage is below 1000 tpa, and substance is not classified as CMR or STOT-RE Cat.1
