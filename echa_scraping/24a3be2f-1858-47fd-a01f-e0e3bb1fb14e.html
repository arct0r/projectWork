    Workers - Hazard for the eyes

    Local effects

    Hazard assessment conclusion
    no hazard identified
    Explanation for hazard conclusion
    [Not publishable]

    Additional information - workers

    In the absence of acute toxicity (lethality) or any adverse effects in any of the available subchronic, reproductive and developmental toxicity studies it is not necessary to quantify DNELs, in accordance with REACH Technical Guidance on information requirements and chemical safety assessment; Part B: Hazard assessment (August 2011).

    Qualitative risk characterisation is required for local skin irritation effects, and semi-quantitative assessment is performed for narcosis.

    Additional information - General Population

    There are no consumer applications of trimethoxy(2-methylpropyl)silane, therefore DNELS for the dermal and inhalation routes are not required.

    In the absence of acute toxicity (lethality) or any adverse effects in any of the available subchronic, reproductive and developmental ,toxicity studies it is not necessary to quantify DNELs for oral exposure, in accordance with REACH Technical Guidance on information requirements and chemical safety assessment; Part B: Hazard assessment (August 2011).
