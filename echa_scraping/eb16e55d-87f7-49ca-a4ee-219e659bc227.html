    Additional information - workers

    Toxicokinetics

    In the absence of toxico-kinetic data of the target chemical4'-aminoazobenzene-4-sulphonic acid; due consideration has been given to its physico-chemical properties. Low vapour pressure and particle size distribution shall ensure that inhalation exposure and absorption is low. BCF value too is very low compared to the threshold that would suggest bio-accumulation. The Danish EPA database predicts the dermal absorption of 4'-aminoazobenzene-4-sulphonic acid to be low (Dermal Abs.mg/cm2/event = 0.00001)

     

    The toxico-kinetic of the read-across chemical indicates absorption, metabolism and rapid excretion out of the living system. Report from other read-across chemical4,4â-Diaminodiphenylsulfone (dapsone)also indicates detoxification and therefore low bio-accumulation potential.

     

    Thus, using the weight of evidence approach; it has been concluded that the bio-accumulation potential of 4'-aminoazobenzene-4-sulphonic acidis likely to be low.

    Acute toxicity:

    The substance 4'-aminoazobenzene-4-sulphonic acid showed toxicity effect by oral route in category IV whereas by dermal route it is non toxic within the concentration levels mentioned in the end points. Also. given the low vapour pressure of 0.0000000000141 Pa, acute toxicity by the inhlation route is not likely.

    Skin & eye irritation:

    The available studies indicate that the substance 4'-aminoazobenzene-4-sulphonic acid was found to be irritating to the skin and eyes

    Skin sensitisation:

    Test substance 4'-aminoazobenzene-4-sulphonic acid was found to be sensitizing to guinea pig skin.

    Additional information - General Population

    DNEL derivation

    4'-aminoazobenzene-4-sulphonic acid exhibit acute toxicity by oral route in category IV whereas by dermal and inhalation route it is non toxic.4'-aminoazobenzene-4-sulphonic acid was found to be irritating to skin and eye. Available data indicates that the chemical does not exhibit genotoxicity and is not a reproductive and developmental toxin within the dose levels mentioned in the end points. 

     

    In the absence of local effects following short-term or long-term exposure, no dose-response data are available and a quantitative dose descriptor is not derived. DNEL values for local exposure are therefore not calculated.

     

    In the absence of acute systemic toxicity, no dose-response data are available and a quantitative dose descriptor is not derived. DNEL values for acute systemic effects are therefore not calculated.

     

    A standard approach to deriving DNEL values in this case would be to use the developmental toxicity dataset and apply assessment factors as described in ECHA guidance documents. The critical endpoint is considered to be the NOAEL of 12 mg/kg by oral category.
