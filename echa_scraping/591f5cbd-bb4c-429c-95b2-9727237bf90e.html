    Workers - Hazard for the eyes

    Local effects

    Hazard assessment conclusion
    medium hazard (no threshold derived)
    Explanation for hazard conclusion
    [Not publishable]

    Additional information - workers

    Magnesium diethyl dicarbonate rapidly hydrolyzes in aqueous environments (t1/2< 1 minute) into carbon dioxide, ethanol and magnesium hydroxide (detailed description in section 5.1.2). Carbon dioxide is a natural gas.

    Toxicity is mediated by itsdegradation products EtOH and Mg(OH)2 and assessed for these products.

    Magnesium hydroxide:

    The most relevant routes of exposure for workers are the dermal and inhalation routes.

    Based on the available acute toxicity data, magnesium hydroxide is not acutely toxic and therefore

    the derivation of a DNELlong-term will be sufficient to control potential risks associated with short-term

    exposures. In addition, based on the available data magnesium hydroxide is not irritating to skin and

    eyes and is not a skin sensitizer. It is also unlikely to be a respiratory irritant or sensitizer. Magnesium

    hydroxide, based on the available data does not appear to elicit local toxicity effects. Therefore a

    derivation of a DNEL for local effects is not considered necessary.

    The lowest reliable NOAEL was derived from a combined 28-day repeated dose toxicity study and

    reproduction/developmental toxicity screening test in rats. The lowest repeated dose toxicity NOAEL

    was derived for males as 1000 mg/kg bw/day. A parental, reproduction and developmental NOAEL of

    at least 1000 mg/kg bw/day was determined for the reproduction/developmental toxicity test. It should

    be noted that no toxicological relevant changes were observed in either study at this dose level which

    was the highest dose tested in this study (van Otterdijk, 2010).
