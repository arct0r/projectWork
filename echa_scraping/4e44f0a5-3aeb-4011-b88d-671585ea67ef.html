    Additional information - workers

    Considering the Guidance on information requirements and chemical safety assessment, R 8 Characterisation of dose [concentration]-response for human health (May 2008) DNELacute and DNELlong-term might be relevant for the chemical safety assessment.

    Based on the absence of findings related to acute toxicity and the absence of a requirement to classify DHA for acute toxicity, no DNELacute is derived.

    As specified in point 3.5, the manufacturing of DHA, the formulation/production of cosmetics, and the use of cosmetics are potential relevant sources for human exposure. As the human health aspects when using DHA in cosmetics is regulated by other legislation, the human exposure for this use is not considered. This is in line with the recommendation given in the Guidance on Registration (May 2008). Considering the uses mentioned in point 3.5 and the absence of a potential secondary exposure via the environment no additional source of exposure of general population exists. Therefore, no general population-DNELs are derived.

    Based on the considerations above, the worker-DNELs are derived. The exposure pattern of workers in the manufacturing and production/formulation of cosmetic products is described as a repeated, long-term exposure. Therefore, worker-DNELslong-term are derived for oral, dermal and inhalation exposure.

    Derivation of worker-DNELlong-term

    An oral NOEL of 1000 mg/kg bw/d was obtained in a subchronic rat study as well as in a developmental toxicity study in rats. This NOEL of 1000 mg/kg bw/d is used to derive the worker-DNELslong-term.

    DHA is not genotoxic, carcinogenic, toxic to reproduction or developmental toxic. DHA is completely bioavailable via the oral route. A mean worst case bioavailability of 33 % is considered for the dermal route. An assessment factor of 4 is included for allometric scaling. An assessment factor of 5 is considered for inter- and intraspecies variability and derivation of the worker-DNELslong-term based on TRGS (BAuA, 1998). As the relevant NOEL is based on a subchronic study whereas the DNELlong-term should account for chronic effects an additional assessment factor of 2 is used (TRGS, BAuA, 1998).

    Considering the overall assessment factor of 40 and the NOEL of 1000 mg/kg bw/d, the worker-DNELlong-termoral is 25 mg/kg bw/d. For the derivation of the worker-DNELlong-termdermal, the DNELlong-termoral is corrected by the difference of bioavailability of the oral and dermal route (25 mg/kg bw/d *100 %/33% = 75 mg/kg bw/d). Consequently, the DNELlong-termdermal is 75 mg/kg bw/d. The worker-DNELlong-terminhalation is 66 mg/m3 based on the NOEC of 2630 mg/m3(NOEC calculated based on the oral NOEL considering the standard breathing volume for the rat (0.38 m3/kg bw for 8 h); the respective DNEL is calculated considering an AF of 5 and an additional correction for the difference between basal caloric demand and caloric demand under light activity. This correction factor derives from the inhalative volumes in 8 hours under the respective conditions (6.7 m3 for base level, 10 m3 for light activity; see Guidance on information requirements and chemical safety assessment, R 8 Characterisation of dose [concentration]-response for human health (May 2008))).
