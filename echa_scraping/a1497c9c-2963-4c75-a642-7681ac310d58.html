    The NOAEL from an  90 day oral toxicity study  was used as a dose descriptor starting point.

    Modification of the dose descriptor starting point was performed via route-to-route extrapolation according to ECHA "Guidance on information requirements and chemical safety assessment, Chapter R.8: Characterisation of dose [concentration]-response for human health" under consideration of exposure conditions of workers (5 days/week vs. 7 days/week during oral gavage study)

    Explanation for the modification of the dose descriptor starting point

    The NOAEL from an  90 day oral toxicity study  was used as a dose descriptor starting point.

    As a worst-case approach, 100% bioavailability was assumedfor both oral and dermal routes in accordance with ECHA "Guidance on information requirements and chemical safety assessment, Chapter R.8: Characterisation of dose [concentration]-response for human health". Therefore no modification of the dose descriptor starting point was performed. Differences in exposure conditions of workers (5 days/week vs. 7 days/week during oral gavage study) have been additionally considered.
