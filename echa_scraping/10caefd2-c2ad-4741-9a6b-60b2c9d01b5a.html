    Additional information - workers

    Worker DNELs are based upon a NOAEL value of 50 mg/kg/day derived from a rat oral dose-feed three-generation reproduction study. A similar NOAEL of 50 mg/kg/day was observed in a mouse oral dose-feed two-generation reproduction study. E.C.H.A. guidance (Guidance on information requirements and chemical safety assessment, Chapter R.8: Characterization of dose [concentration]-response for human health) was applied in the derivation of Interspecies Scalling and Assessment Factors. Worker DNEL systemic effects are believed to be protective for potential local effects. Worker dermal DNEL local effects is based on the outcome of a mouse Local Lymph Node Assay.

    Additional information - General Population

    General Population systemic effects DNELs are based upon the NOAEL of 50 mg/kg/day derived from a rat oral dose-feed three-generation reproduction study sponsored by the U.S. National Toxicology Program. This NOAEL value is similar to the NOAEL observed in a mouse oral dose-feed two-generation reporduction study. E.C.H.A. guidance (Guidance on information requirements and chemical safety assessment, Chapter R.8: Characterization of dose [concentration]-response for human health) was followed in the derivation of Interspecies Scalling and Assessment Factors. The DNEL Dermal acute/short-term local effects is based upon the results of a mouse Local Lymph Node Assay with positive sensitization findings at a 30% concentration. The General Population systemic effects DNELS are believed to offer sufficient protection for any other potential local adverse effects from any unlikely exposure to 2 -Acetone polymer with phenol (BPA-Tars).
