    Explanation for the modification of the dose descriptor starting point

    A systemic NOAEL of 150 mg/kg/day is based on a 28-day oral rat gavage study. Oral absorption rat - oral/dermal absorption human: Low, 10% dermal absorption is asumed, based the physical-chemical properties: molecular weight of 230.3, log Pow of 4.9, water solubility of < 0.6 mg/L mg/l and vapor pressure 300 Pa (0.3) kPa) at 20 °C. Therefore, 150 mg/kg bw/day/0.1 = 1500 mg/kg bw/day dermal dose descriptor. This can further be corrected for change from 7d/wk dosing to 5d/wk exposure (devided by 0.714) resulting to modified NOAEC starting point of 2100 mg/kg bw/day.

    Workers - Hazard for the eyes

    Local effects

    Hazard assessment conclusion
    no hazard identified
    Explanation for hazard conclusion
    [Not publishable]

    Additional information - workers

    Exposures can be expected to be limited and very well controlled, as:

    - The substance has only industrial use as peroxide in the production of polymers in which it will be completely consumed. There are no consumer or secondary exposures possible.

    - The substance is thermally instable and has a SADT of 20 deg.C, and a flash point of 14 deg.C. The product is stored in temperature controlled facilities at about -15 deg C. Also transport needs to take place under closely regulated conditions.

    - The substance is irritating and classified as sensitising to the skin. Consequently measures are taken to preclude exposures to workers.

    - The substance has a low vapour pressure of 0.3 hPa at 20 deg.C.
