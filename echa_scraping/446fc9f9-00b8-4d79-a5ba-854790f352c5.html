    Explanation for hazard conclusion

    Additional information - workers

    The substance is not classified for human health and results from available studies indicate the substance to be of low toxicity.

    No adverse effects, systemic or local, were observed in acute studies conducted on the substance,9-Dodecenoic acid, methyl ester, or in studies conducted on a structural analogue read-across substance, 9-decenoic acid, methyl ester. No adverse effects were observed in subacute and subchronic oral toxicity studies with the read-across substance, 9-decenoic acid, methyl ester, at the highest tested dose (1000 mg/kg bw/day).

     

    No inhalation study data is available on the substance but the substance is considered to be of low toxicity based on available data and to present a low hazard to human health. In addition, the substance is also considered to be of low volatility based on the low vapour pressure (0.99 Pa at 25Â°C) and lack of hazardous physic-chemical properties.

     

    Based on the above, it is considered that DNELs are not required to be derived and that a qualitative assessment is sufficient to assess the risk to workers.
