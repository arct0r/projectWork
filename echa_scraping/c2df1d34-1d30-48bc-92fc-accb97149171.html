    Workers - Hazard for the eyes

    Local effects

    Hazard assessment conclusion
    [Empty]
    Explanation for hazard conclusion
    [Not publishable]

    Additional information - workers

    Workplace exposure to silicic acid, aluminium salt is neither regulated on the EU level nor in any of the member states. Therefore, the limits of inhalation exposure to silicic acid, aluminium salt at the workplace are based on the general limit values for dust (TRGS 900, June 2008): The existing OEL for dust is 3 mg/mÂ³ (alveolar fraction) and 10 mg/mÂ³ (respirable fraction). It is proposed to adopt the more critical value of 3 mg/mÂ³ as DNEL. This approach is in compliance with the ECHA "Guidance on information requirements and chemical safety assessment. Chapter R.8: Characterisation of dose [concentration]-response for human health", May 2008.

    Silicic acid, aluminium salt is corrosive to eyes. However, no dose response data are available to derive DNELs. Therefore this hazard is addressed in a qualitative way by the advice to wear suitable personal protective equipment, including goggles, gloves and protective clothing.

    Additional information - General Population

    Silicic acid, aluminium salt is used in inclusion into or onto matrices. Therefore a risk potential to the general population is not expected at all.

    Since exposure of the general public is not relevant, DNELs for the general population were not derived.
