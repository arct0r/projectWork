    Workers - Hazard for the eyes

    Local effects

    Hazard assessment conclusion
    hazard unknown (no further information necessary)
    Explanation for hazard conclusion
    [Not publishable]

    Additional information - workers

    Lysozyme may cause allergy or asthma symptoms or breathing difficulties if inhaled; according to CLP regulation (EC1272/2008) Lysozyme is classified as Resp. Sens. 1, H334.

    Therefore, Lysozyme is associated to an high hazard profile (i.e., category 1 and 2 carcinogens, potent category 3 carcinogens, category 1, 2 and 3 mutagens, very (acutely) toxic substances, strong corrosives, extreme/strong skin sensitizers and respiratory sensitizers) and a very high level of containment, automatic dosing/feeding to the process, and appropriate PPE are recommended in occupational settings in order to avoid exposure.
