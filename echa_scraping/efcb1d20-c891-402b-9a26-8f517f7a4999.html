    Additional information - workers

    Kinetics (absorption figures for oral, dermal and inhalation route of exposure):

    No data on absorption are available. According to Chapter R.8 of REACH Guidance on information requirements and chemical safety assessment, it is proposed in the absence of route-specific information on the starting route, to include a default factor of 2 in the case of oral-to-inhalation extrapolation.

     
    Acute toxicity:

    N,N-dimethylacrylamide is classified for acute oral and dermal toxicity. As no high peak exposures occur for the systemic oral and dermal routes, derivation of a DNELacute for these routes is not necessary.

    N,N-dimethylacrylamide is not classified for acute inhalation toxicity, and, therefore, derivation of a DNELacute for inhalation is not necessary.


    Irritation/corrosion and sensitisation:

    The substance is not irritating to the skin, causes severe damage to eyes (Cat. 1). The substance is classified as sensitising to skin (Cat. 1B).

     
    Repeated dose toxicity:

    The study considered for long-term systemic inhalatory DNEL derivation of N,N-dimethylacrylamide is a 28-day repeated oral dose toxicity study similar to OECD guideline 407 carried out using 6-week old SPF Sprague-Dawley rats, in which N,N-dimethylacrylamide was given to rats by oral gavage. The doses were 0 (water for injection: control group), 5, 20 and 80 mg/kg. Also, a 2-week recovery period after completion of administration was established for some of the control group and some of the 80 mg/kg group (6 males and 6 females per group), and the reversibility of the toxic changes was evaluated.

    Test substance administration had no observable effect on the general condition, function tests, or grip strength. The detailed examination of the general condition throughout the administration period revealed loss of coat luster, unkempt or soiled coat in males of the 80 mg/kg group, low or moderate urine volumes in males and females in the 80 mg/kg group, and infrequent rearing in females in the 80 mg/kg group. There was a low level of motor activity in males and females in the 80 mg/kg group. Throughout the administration period the body weight in males and females in the 80 mg/kg group was lower than that in the control group, and the body weight gain was also low. In addition, feed intake was low in males and females in the 80 mg/kg group on administration day 7, moreover it was low among males throughout the administration period. Urinalysis revealed low osmotic pressure in females in the 80 mg/kg group. Hematological tests revealed low red blood cell counts, hemoglobin levels and hematocrit values, high reticulocyte values, high neutrophil counts and prolonged activated partial thromboplastin times in females in the 80 mg/kg group. Moreover, the red blood cell counts, hemoglobin levels and hematocrit values tended to be low in males in the 80 mg/kg group. Blood chemistry revealed low creatinine levels in males in the 80 mg/kg group. Of the organ weights, there were low thymus gland weights in males in the 80 mg/kg group. Autopsy revealed dark red lesions in the glandular stomach in 3/6 females in the 80 mg/kg group. Histopathology revealed thymus gland atrophy and increased eosinophilic bodies in the renal tubules in males in the 80 mg/kg group, accentuated extramedullary hematopoiesis of the spleen and erosion of the glandular stomach in females in the 80 mg/kg group, and increased BB-positive granules in the spleen in both males and females in the 80 mg/kg group. During the recovery period, many of the changes observed in the administration period either disappeared or decreased.

    In conclusion, the no observed effect level of the test substance under the conditions of this study was estimated to be 20 mg/kg bw/day in males and females.


    The study considered for long-term systemic dermal DNEL derivation of N,N-dimethylacrylamide is the 13-week repeated dermal dose toxicity GLP study comparable to OECD guideline 411. Groups of ten male and ten female Sprague-Dawley rats were dosed with N,N-dimethylacrylamide under occlusive conditions. Initially, the doses were set at 10, 200 and 500 mg/kg bw/day. However, after six days of administration, it was evident that the highest dose exceeded the maximum tolerated dose since it resulted in overt systemic toxicity (i.e., convulsions, dramatically reduced body weight, body weight gain and food consumption) and two deaths. Therefore, the mid and high dose levels were reduced to 75 and 250 mg/kg bw/day, respectively. The animals that died were replaced with naive animals and the surviving high dose rats were allowed 2 days of rest/recovery prior to administration of the new dose level (i.e., 250 mg/kg bw/day). Salient clinical signs associated with dose levels at or above 250 mg/kg bw/day, especially among females, consisted of hypothermia (after the first dose only), hyperexcitability, piloerection, hunched posture, emaciation, as well as convulsions and death of 2 females. No treatment-related signs of dermal irritation were observed at the application site. No test article-related changes were observed in the tests (grip strength, foot splay and rotorod performance) used to evaluate peripheral neuropathy.

    Food consumption was significantly reduced during the first week of treatment (i.e., prior to reducing the dose levels) in the mid and high dose groups. Following reduction of the doses, food consumption was unaffected except for a single reduction noted during week 8 in the high dose males. Treatment-related decreases in body weight and/or body weight gain were observed in the high dose rats for the duration of the study.

    Treatment-related changes observed in hematology parameters consisted of decreased total erythrocyte count, accompanied by increases in mean corpuscular volume, mean corpuscular hemoglobin and platelets in females and increases in relative and absolute mature neutrophil counts in both sexes. Alterations in select clinical chemistry and urology parameters, organ weights and organ-to-body weight ratios were detected in the high dose animals. Test article-related histopathological changes were confined to the kidneys of mid and high dose males and consisted of minimal nephropathy and renal pelvis dilatation. No histopathological evidence of neurotoxicity was observed in the test article-treated rats.

    In conclusion, based on the results from this study, the NOAEL is 10 mg/kg bw/day and the LOAEL is 75 mg/kg bw/day.

     

    Mutagenicity:

    The test substance was negative in both an Ames test and in an HPRT assay. The test substance was positive in both an in vitro chromosomal aberration test and a mouse lymphoma assay. The positive result in the chromosomal abberation test showed a slight structural chromosome aberration inducibility. The positive result in the lymphoma assay was indicative of in vitro clastogenicity. The in vivo micronucleus test was negative, therefore, based in these available studies it can be concluded that N,N-Dimethylacrylamide is not mutagenic.

     

    Reproduction toxicity:

    In the GLP compliant combined reproduction/developmental toxicity screening test in Wistar rats (according to OECD 421), the NOAEL for reproductive performance, fertility and developmental toxicity was 30 mg/kg bw/day (the highest dose tested). Since this value is higher than the NOAEL for general systemic toxicity in this study, no DNEL has to be derived for developmental and reproductive toxicity.

     

    DNEL derivation:

    For short-term toxicity, no DNEL needs to be derived for all routes of exposure.

     

    For long-term oral toxicity, regarding systemic effects, a NOEL of 20 mg/kg bw was observed in the 28-day repeated oral dose toxicity study similar to OECD guideline 407.

    For long-term dermal toxicity, regarding systemic effects, a NOAEL of 10 mg/kg bw was observed in a 13-week subchronic repeated dose toxicity in rats (OECD 411). However, due to the wide dose spacing and the fact that in the oral subacute repeated dose toxicity study in rats a NOEL of 20 mg/kg bw was determined and dermal and oral absorption is expected to be similar (also evidenced by the classification for acute oral and dermal toxicity), the NOAEL of 10 mg/kg bw is regared as too conservative as starting point for the DNEL derivation. Therefore the LOAEL of 75 mg/kg bw is used and converted in a hypothetical NOAEL of 25 mg/kg bw by using a safety assessment factor of 3.

    Long-term inhalation toxicity data is not available.

     

    Worker DNELs:

    Long-term –inhalation, systemic effects (based on a 28-day repeated oral dose toxicity study similar to OECD guideline 407):

    Description Value Remark
    Step 1) Relevant dose-descriptor NOEL: 20 mg/kg bw/day Based on clinical signs of toxicity, changes in behavior, body weight, food consumption, organ weights and effected parameters in urinalysis, hematology, clinical biochemistry and gross as well as histopathology at the next higher dose level of 80 mg/kg bw/d.
    Step 2) Modification of starting point

    Corrected inhalatory NOAEC worker = oral NOEL rat x (1/(0.38 m3/kg bw (rat, 8h)) x (50%/100%) x ((7d/wk)/(5 d/wk)) *((6.7 m3/person 8h)/(10 m3/person 8h))

    		 A correction for the exposure route needs to be considered because of a route-to-route extrapolation. First, the units of NOEL (from the rat study) and NOAEC as the starting point for the human DNEL differ. This is corrected by relating the oral NOEL of the rat study to the respiration rate of the rat during 8h (the rat respires 0.38 m3 in 8h; 8h were selected according to the daily working time of the worker) (factor 1/0.38). Second, the route-specific absorption rate has to be corrected, because for the inhalation route a double absorption is assumed (factor 50/100). A correction for exposure conditions is needed because in the study animals were exposed at 7d/wk, but for a worker with a normal work week a 5-day exposure is assumed (factor 7/5). Furthermore, a correction for the respiration rate must be considered. In reference to an increased respiration rate under light physical exertion, an increased respiration rate of 10 m3 for workers is assumed in comparison to a respiration rate of 6.7 m3 at rest (factor 6.7/10). No correction for the absorption rate is needed as there are no data on kinetics that would prove a species difference.
    Modified dose-descriptor 20 x (1/0.38) x (50/100) x (7/5) x (6.7/10) = 24.7 mg/m3
    Step 3) Assessment factors    
    AF 1 (dose response relationship) 1 ECHA REACH Guidance (starting point for the DNEL calculation is a NOEL)
    AF 2 (exposure duration) 6 ECHA REACH Guidance (default assessment factor for subacute studies)
    AF 3 (interspecies differences) 1 ECHA REACH Guidance (no allometric scaling needed)
    AF 4 (other interspecies factors) 2.5 ECHA REACH Guidance (default assessment factor)
    AF 5 (intraspecies factors) 5 ECHA REACH Guidance (default assessment factor for worker)
    AF 6 (quality database) and AF 7 (remaining uncertainties) both 1 ECHA REACH Guidance (good/standard quality of database) and ECHA REACH Guidance
    DNEL Value
      24.7 / (1 x 6 x 1 x 2.5 x 5 x 1 x 1) = 24.7 / 75 =0.329 mg/m3

     

    Long-term - inhalation, local effects:

    No data are available based on which a DNEL for local effects can be derived. There are also no data that suggest that the substance may cause local effects by inhalation exposure.

     

    Long-term – dermal, systemic effects (based on 90 day repeated dose dermal toxicity study with rats):

    Description Value Remark
    Step 1) Relevant dose-descriptor LOAEL: 75 mg/kg bw/day Based on minimal renal toxicity, alterations in clinical pathology parameters and significant alterations in body weight parameters.
    Step 2) Modification of starting point Corrected dermal NOAEL worker = dermal LOAEL rat/3 * ((7d/wk)/(5d/wk))

    A safety factor of 3 is used for conversion of the LOAEL in a NOAEL.

    A correction for exposure conditions is needed because in the study animals were exposed at 7d/wk, but for a worker with a normal work week a 5-day exposure is assumed (factor 7/5). No correction for the dermal absorption rate is needed because as there are no data on kinetics that would prove a species difference. 
    Modified dose-descriptor

    75/3 x (7/5) = 35 mg/kg bw/day
    Step 3) Assessment factors    
    AF 1 (dose response relationship) 1 ECHA REACH Guidance (starting point for the DNEL calculation is a NOAEL)
    AF 2 (exposure duration) 2 ECHA REACH Guidance (default assessment factor for subchronic studies)
    AF 3 (interspecies differences) 4 ECHA REACH Guidance (default assessment factor for rat)
    AF 4 (other interspecies factors) 2.5 ECHA REACH Guidance (default assessment factor)
    AF 5 (intraspecies factors) 5 ECHA REACH Guidance (default assessment factor for worker)
    AF 6 (quality database) and AF 7 (remaining uncertainties) both 1 ECHA REACH Guidance (good/standard quality of database) and ECHA REACH Guidance
    DNEL Value
      35 / (1 x 2 x 4 x 2.5 x 5 x 1 x 1) = 35 / 100 = 0.35 mg/kg bw/day

     

    Long-term - dermal, local effects:

    N,N-dimethylacrylamide is classified for acute dermal toxicity (Cat. 3), therefore, the substance is assigned to the moderate hazard band (H311) in accordance with ECHA Guidance on information requirements and chemical safety assessment Part E: Risk Characterisation (2016). Appropriate qualitative risk management measures should be implemented to avoid exposure.

    Long-term – inhalation, systemic effects (based on subacute oral repeated dose toxicity study in rats conducted similar to OECD guideline 407):

    Description Value Remark
    Step 1) Relevant dose-descriptor NOEL: 20 mg/kg bw/day Based on clinical signs of toxicity, changes in behavior, body weight, food consumption, organ weights and effected parameters in urinalysis, hematology, clinical biochemistry and gross as well as histopathology at the next higher dose level of 80 mg/kg bw/d.
    Step 2) Modification of starting point

    Corrected inhalatory NOAEC gen.pop. = oral NOEL rat x (1/(1.35 m3/kg bw rat, 24h)) x (50%/100%) = 10.5 mg/kg bw/day

    No correction for the absorption rate is needed because there are no data on kinetics that would prove a species difference.  A route-to-route extrapolation has to be corrected for the route-specific absorption rate, because for the inhalation route a double absorption is assumed (factor 50/100). Furthermore, the route of exposure has to be corrected in that the oral NOAEL of the rat study must be related to respiration rate of the rat during 24 h exposure as it corresponds to the expected exposure time for the general population (the rat respires 1.35 m3 in 24 h; 24 h were selected here according to the daily exposure time of the general population) (factor 1/1.35).
    Modified dose-descriptor 20 x (1/1.35) x (50/100) = 7.4 mg/m3

    Long-term - dermal, local effects:

    N,N-dimethylacrylamide is classified for acute dermal toxicity (Cat. 3) and as skin sensitizing (Cat. 1B), therefore, the substance is assigned to the moderate hazard band in accordance with ECHA Guidance on information requirements and chemical safety assessment Part E: Risk Characterisation (2016). Appropriate qualitative risk management measures should be implemented to avoid exposure.
