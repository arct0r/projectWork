    Explanation for the modification of the dose descriptor starting point
    The inhalation DNEL of 0.13 mg/m3 was calculated from a NOAEL of 1.4 mg/kg bw/day for the target organ, pancreas, in male rats (Ciofalo 1992). The NOAEL was adjusted to correct for the difference between respiratory rates under standard conditions and under conditions of light activity as follows (ECHA, 2008, at p. 68): corrected inhalatory NOAEC = oral NOAEL x [1 ÷ sRVrat] x [ABSoral-rat ÷ ABSinh-human] x [sRVhuman ÷ wRV] corrected inhalatory NOAEC = 1.4 mg/kg bw/day x [1 ÷ 0.8 L/min/kg bw] x [100% ÷ 100%] x [6.7 m3 ÷ 10 m3] = 1.17 mg/m3 A composite assessment factor of 9 was applied to the corrected NOAEC to derive the inhalation DNEL. The individual assessment factors consisted of the following: Interspecies = 3 (ECETOC, 2003, at p. 23). Intraspecies = 3. This value was applied as the standard default value to account for differences between workers for system effects (ECCETOC, 2003, at p. 23). Exposure duration = 1. The corrected inhalatory NOAEC is based on for the target organ at study termination from a two-year carcinogenicity study (Ciofalo, 1992). Dose-response = not applied. Quality of whole database = not applied. The database is complete.

The individual assessment factors consisted of the following: Interspecies = 10. A standard default of 10 was applied. Intraspecies = 5. This value was applied as the standard default value to account for differences in the general population (ECETOC, 2003, at p. 23). Exposure duration = 1. The BMDL10 is based on the decreased body weight at 43 weeks from a two-year carcinogenicity study. Dose-response = not applied. The DNEL was derived using a BMDL10. Therefore, no assessment factor was necessary. Quality of whole database = not applied. The database is complete.
