    Additional information - General Population

    According to Article 13 of Regulation (EC) No. 1907/2006 "General Requirements for Generation of Information on Intrinsic Properties of substances", information on intrinsic properties of substances may be generated by means other than tests e.g. from information from structurally related substances (grouping or read-across), provided that conditions set out in Annex XI are met. Annex XI, "General rules for adaptation of this standard testing regime set out in Annexes VII to X states that substances whose physicochemical, toxicological and ecotoxicological properties are likely to be similar or follow a regular pattern as a result of structural similarity may be considered as a group, or category of substances. This avoids the need to test every substance for every endpoint". Since the group concept is applied to the members of the LCAE category, data will be generated from data for reference source substance(s) to avoid unnecessary animal testing. Additionally, once the group concept is applied, substances will be classified and labelled on this basis. A report on read across is added to this dossier.

    Studies show that tetradecyl laurate and other analogues of the long-chain aliphatic ester (LCAE) category are not acutely toxic, skin or eye irritant, snesitizing or genotoxic. Therefore, tetradecyl laurate, as these LCAE analogues, does not need to be classified or labelled as a potential human hazard according to the 2nd ATP of the Regulation (EC) No.1272/2008 (CLP).
