    Additional information - workers

    The substance is of low acute toxicity following oral and dermal exposure. The substance is not classifiable for skin and eye irritating properties, but was found to be a skin sensitizer in a LLNA which did not give information on dose-response. On these bases no acute DNELs are required while no DNEL could be calculated for acute and long-term dermal (or inhalation) local effects.

    The genotoxic potential of the substance was evaluated in-vitro, with all endpoints resulting negative. On this basis the substance is considered to not possess genotoxic potential.

    Although historically considered a potential human carcinogen because of positive results in dermal non-guideline animal studies, a more recent and fully compliant inhalation study in rats with an analogous substance showed no carcinogenic potential by this route of exposure, more relevant to actual worker exposure. On this basis the toxicological endpoint obtained in a fully compliant 28-day study, under the worst condition of oral gavage, was chosen as the more appropriate for DNELs setting. The study set a LOAEL at 15 mg/kg bw/day because of minimal anaemic response, adaptive microscopic liver changes and isolated increased bilirubin levels extended to rats at this dose level.

    An additional assessment factor of 3 was used to extrapolate from the LOAEL to a NOAEL. In addition, an additional assessment factor of 3 was applied to take into account the lack of data on reproductive toxicity.

    Additional information - General Population

    The substance is of low acute toxicity following oral and dermal exposure and therefore no DNELs for acute systemic effects would be required. However, as exposure of the general population is expected to potentially occur on a sporadic occasional basis, acute DNELs for each route of exposure (i.e. dermal, inhalation and oral) have been set, using the same toxicological endpoint used for workerâs DNELs calculation. As the starting point is a LOAEL obtained in a fully compliant oral repeat dose (28-day) study, the starting point has not been adjusted for duration of exposure as a worst case assumption.

    Just for completeness, also long-term systemic DNELs were calculated, as for workers, although long-term exposure of the general population is considered unlikely.
