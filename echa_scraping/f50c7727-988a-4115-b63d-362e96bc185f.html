    As a worst case the DNEL for systemic effects via the inhalation route is determined on the basis of route-to-route extrapolation from the oral NOAEL of 100 mg/kg bw/day. The following correction was made to the NOAEL(oral) to derive the NOAEC(inhalation):  Correction respiratory rate and volume (rat to human consumer) (24 hour) 1/1.15 mÂ³/kg bw. Therefore the corrected NOAEC for repeated-dose systemic effects via the inhalation route is: [100 mg/kg bw/day*(1/1.15 mÂ³/kg bw)] = 87 mg/mÂ³.

    The corrected NOAEC for repeated-dose systemic effects via the inhalation route is also the basis of the infrequent exposure DNEL for the registered substance. However, due to the infrequent nature of the exposure, the assessment factor for differences in duration of exposure (assessment factor of 2) can be omitted. Therefore, the overall assessment factor is reduced to 25. The inhalation DNEL for infrequent exposure is therefore 87 / 25 = 3.48 mg/m3.
