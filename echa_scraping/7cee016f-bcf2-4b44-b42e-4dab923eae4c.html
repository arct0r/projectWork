    Additional information - workers

    The long-term DNELs are based on a subacute 28 day oral gavage study in rats (Pittermann, 1992).

    The oral subacute NOAEL for Fatty acids, C16-18, 2-ethylhexyl esters was found to be 1000 mg/kg bw/d.

    Dermal DNEL:

    QSAR based dermal permeability regarding molecular weight, log Pow and water solubility, calculated a very low dermal absorption for the read across substance 2-ethylhexyl palmitate (CAS No. 29806 -73 -3, see section 7.1.2, Danish EPA, 2010). Therefore, assuming a dermal absorption of 10% for the very similar substance C16-18, 2-ethylhexyl esters (CAS No. 91031 -48 -0) displays a sufficient conservative approach.

    Assessment factors were used according to the "Guidance on information requirements and chemical safety assessment Chapter R.8: Characterisation of dose [concentration]-response for human health", ECHA, May 2008.

    Inhalation DNEL worker:

    The NAEC was determined by calculating from oral exposure to 8h inhalation exposure by factor 0.38 and allometric scaling from rat to human worker by factor 0.67 according to the "Guidance on information requirements and chemical safety assessment Chapter R.8: Characterisation of dose [concentration]-response for human health", ECHA, May 2008
