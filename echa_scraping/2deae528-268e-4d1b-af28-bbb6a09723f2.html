    Workers - Hazard for the eyes

    Local effects

    Hazard assessment conclusion
    medium hazard (no threshold derived)
    Explanation for hazard conclusion
    [Not publishable]

    Additional information - workers

    Workers might be exposed to disodium metasilicate during manufacture, processing or filling in appropriate containers.

    The dermal as well as the inhalation DNEL for workers were determined using route-to-route extrapolation.

    The existing OEL (TRGS 900, June 2008) for dust is 3 mg/m3 (alveolar fraction) and 10 mg/m3 (respirable fraction). The more critical value of 3 mg/m3 is based on effects (5% elevation of the prevalence for chronic bronchitis and lung emphysema) observed in epidemiological studies taking into account a typical dust density at the workplace of 2.5 mg/cm3.

    The derived DNEL for inhalation is higher than the existing OEL for dust, therefore long-term systemic effects caused by disodium metasilicate are not expected to occur as long as the OEL is complied with. However, because of the high alkalinity of disodium metasilicate, local effects to the skin, eyes and respiratory tract have to be accounted for.

    Local effects

    Hazard assessment conclusion
    medium hazard (no threshold derived)
    Explanation for hazard conclusion
    [Not publishable]

    Additional information - General Population

    The direct and indirect skin contact, inhalation and oral routes as well as the accidental overexposure (eye or oral exposure) were identified and assessed by HERA (2005) as relevant consumer exposure scenarios for disodium metasilicate. The primary exposure results from dermal contact. Short-term exposure to dust may occur by the use of products in powder/granular form only, other application forms are of no concern with respect to inhalative exposure. Oral exposure for consumers usually plays a minor role.
