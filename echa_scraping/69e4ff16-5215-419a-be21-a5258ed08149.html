    Explanation for hazard conclusion

    Explanation for hazard conclusion

    No study on acute inhalation toxicity is available for Bisphenol TMC which is a solid with a low vapour pressure under normal ambient conditions. The available acute toxicity studies for the oral and dermal route of exposure show only unspecific and slight effects or no findings at all, respectively. LD50-values > 2000 mg/kg bw indicate no substantial acute toxicity of Bisphenol TMC. Therefore, acute inhalation toxicity is unlikely to be an endpoint of concern for the substance and "no hazard identified" is concluded.

    Explanation for the modification of the dose descriptor starting point

    No study with repeated dermal exposure is available. The acute dermal study reveal a comparable low toxicity as determined for the oral route. There is no indication that dermal absorption is higher than oral absorption. Nevertheless the lipophilicity as well as the observed signs of systemic toxicity in the acute dermal toxicity study with rats (bad general condition, bloody mouths) give some indication of at least some dermal absorption. Therefore the results of the oral toxicity studies are used for assessing the systemic toxicity after dermal exposure.
