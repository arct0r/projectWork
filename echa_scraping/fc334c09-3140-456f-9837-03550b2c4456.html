    Additional information - workers

    A single 28 day repeat dose combine reproductive and developmental toxicity study was available on rats. As the only study available, Zmarowski (2012) was used as the starting point for long term systemic DNEL calculations. The oral NOEAL was suitable for extrapolation for calculating both inhalation and dermal DNELâs. An additional safety factor of 3 was applied to account for the limited information on chronic, reproductive and developmental toxicity was available.

    The substance is harmful following oral administration, with an LD50 of 1717 mg/kg, and is of low toxicity following dermal exposure, LD50 > 2000 mg/kg. Particle size distribution indicates that any particles deposited in the nasopharyngeal region would pass rapidly into the gastrointestinal tract. On this basis an acute inhalation DNEL for systemic effects is considered not necessary. Similarly due to the low acute toxicity and lack of local or systemic effects from dermal exposure, the acute dermal DNEL for systemic effects is also not considered necessary.

    In vivo testing with 3,5-dimethylpyrazole did not produced adverse local effects in animals.The substance is not irritating to the delicate mucous membrane of the eye. It is not irritating to the skin and has no skin sensitisation potential. No local effects are expected to occur. Therefore both long and short term DNELâs were not required.

    Based on in vitro studies, 3,5-dimethylpyrazole is considered not to possess any genotoxic potential.
