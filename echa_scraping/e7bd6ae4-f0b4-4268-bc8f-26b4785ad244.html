    Explanation for the modification of the dose descriptor starting point

    The dose descriptor start point is modified from a NOAEL by oral administration in rats (mg/kg/day) to a No Observed Adverse Effect Concentration (NOAEC) in humans by inhalation exposure (mg/m3) before Adjustment Factors (AFs) can be applied to determine the DNEL. The conversion in accordance with ECHA Guidance on Information Requirements and Chemical Safety Assessment Chapter R8 is as follows (in case of Workers exposed 8 hours/day):

    NOAEC = oral NOAEL x 1/sRVrat x ABSoral rat / ABSinh human x sRVhuman / wRV

    Where: sRV = Standard respiratory volume (0.38 m3 rat and 6.7 m3 human)

                ABS = Absorption

                wRV = Worker respiratory volume (10 m3)

    Therefore: NOAEC = 500 x 1/0.38 x 1/1 x 6.7/10

    NOAEC = 881.6 mg/m3

    Explanation for the modification of the dose descriptor starting point

    The dermal systemic acute DNEL has been extrapolated from the systemic dermal long term DNEL. In the absence of reliable experimental data, an acute DNEL can by default be set at 1-5 times the long term DNEL. The factor used to extrapolate the dermal systemic acute DNEL from the systemic dermal long term DNEL is based on a qualitative assessment of acute toxicity and skin irritation and corrosion tests and a dermal combined short-term repeat dose and reproductive toxicity screening study. Therefore, it is considered appropriate to modify the systemic DNEL by the factor of 3 based on the dermal irritation observed in the dermal combined short-term repeat dose and reproductive toxicity screening study.

    Additional information - workers

    The DNELs for Lithium stearate have been calculated based on the NOAEL from the OECD 422 combined repeat dose and reproductive and developmental screening study on Fatty acids C16-18 lithium salts. The source substance has been selected as this substance is the most structurally similar member of the lithium salts of monocarboxylic acids C14-C22 category, for which suitable data are available, compared to the target substance. See category and read across justification attached to IUCLID.
