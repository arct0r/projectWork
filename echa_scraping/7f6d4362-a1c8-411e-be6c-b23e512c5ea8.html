    Local effects

    Hazard assessment conclusion
    high hazard (no threshold derived)
    Explanation for hazard conclusion

    Additional information - workers

    General

    DNEL derivation is performed under consideration of the recommendations of ECHA REACH Guidance (2010) and ECETOC (2010). In view of the data used for evaluation, the "quality of whole database factors" and "dose-response factors" are considered to be a value of 1.  

    Long term exposure- systemic effect

    Inhalation exposure

    A subchronic repeated dose toxicity test with the reaction product of oleyl alcohol with polyphosphoric acid is not available. Consequently, a subacute repeated dose toxicity test with the test item was used to derive the worker DNEL (long-term inhalation exposure).

    The 28 -day study revealed a NOAEL of > 1000 mg/kg bw/day.

    Correction of the dose descriptor:

    Oral NOAEL: > 1000 mg/kg bw/day

    sRV(rat): 0.38 m3/ kg bw (8 hours) [standard respiratory volume of the rat]

    ABS oral (rat)/ ABS inhalation (human): 0.5 [ratio of oral absorption in the rat to inhalative absorption in the human] sRV (human)/ wRV (human): 6.7 m3/ 10 m3= 0.67 m3 [ratio of human standard respiratory volume to worker respiratory volume]

    The oral NOAEL > 1000 mg/kg bw/ day is converted in an inhalation NOAEC > 882 mg/ m3.  

    Calculation of the worker DNEL:

    Corrected inhalatory NOAEC for worker: > 882 mg/ m3

    Assessment factor for exposure duration (subacute to chronic): 6

    Assessment factor for intraspecies differences (worker): 3 

    Assessment factor for other interspecies differences: 2.5

    Worker DNEL (inhalation exposure) = 882 mg/m3/ (1 x 6 x 1 x 3 x 2.5 x 1 x 1) = 882 / 45 = 19.6 mg/ m3  

    Dermal exposure

    A subchronic repeated dose toxicity test with the reaction product of oleyl alcohol with polyphosphoric acid is not available. Consequently, a subacute repeated dose toxicity test with the test item was used to derive the worker DNEL (long-term inhalation exposure). A 28-day repeated dose oral toxicity study revealed a NOAEL of > 1000 mg/kg bw/day.

    Considering the appropriate assessment factors, the worker DNEL (long-term dermal exposure) is calculated as follows:  

    Correction of the dose descriptor:

    Dose descriptor of relevant study: > 10 000 mg/kg bw/day (NOAEL x 10; assuming 10 % dermal absorption)

    Assessment factor for exposure duration (subacute to chronic): 6

    Allometric scaling factor (rat to human): 4

    Assessment factor for other interspecies differences: 2.5

    Assessment factor for intraspecies differences (worker): 3 

    Taking the above mentioned assessment factors into account, the following worker DNEL is:

    Worker DNEL (dermal exposure) = 10 000 mg/kg bw/day / (1 x 6 x 4 x 2.5 x 3 x 1 x 1) = 10 000 / 180 = 55.6 mg/kg bw/day  

    Acute/ short term exposure- systemic effect

    Inhalation

    There is no indication for acute systemic toxicity of the reaction product of oleyl alcohol with polyphosphoric acid. The substance is not classified for inhalation toxicity. The vapour pressure of the high viscous liquid is negligible. Taken together, its physicochemical properties exclude inhalative exposure. Also no peak exposure is expected. Therefore no DNEL is required.

    Dermal

    In an acute dermal toxicity study with the reaction product of oleyl alcohol with polyphosphoric acid according EU method B.3 and OECD 402, a LD50 value of > 2000 mg/kg bw was obtained. Thus, the test material is not classified and labelled for acute dermal toxicity, according to Directive 67/548/EEC (DSD) and Regulation (EC) No 1272/2008 (CLP). Therefore no DNEL was derived.

    Acute and long term exposure- local effect

    Respiratory irritation

    There is no indication for adverse local effects from acute/subacute oral and acute dermal toxicity tests and tests on skin irritation/corrosion. Therefore, the substance is neither classified for acute toxicity nor for skin irritation/corrosion according to Directive 67/548/EEC (DSD) and Regulation (EC) No 1272/2008 (CLP). However, the substance is classified as eye irritant cat. 1 according to Directive 67/548/EEC (DSD) and Regulation (EC) No 1272/2008 (CLP). The vapour pressure of the high viscous liquid is negligible. Taken together, its physicochemical properties exclude respiratory exposure. Therefore no DNEL is required.

    Skin irritation

    The test item is not classified for skin irritation/corrosion and skin sensitization according to Directive 67/548/EEC (DSD) and Regulation (EC) No 1272/2008 (CLP). Therefore no DNEL was derived.

    Eye Irritation

    The reaction product of oleyl alcohol with polyphosphoric acid is classified as eye irritant category 1 according to Directive 67/548/EEC (DSD) and Regulation (EC) No 1272/2008 (CLP).

    Additional information - General Population

    General population is not intended to be exposed to the reaction product of oleyl alcohol with polyphosphoric acid via inhalation, dermal or oral route. Therefore, no DNEL (systemic or local, long-term or short-term) is derived for general population. The test item is classified for eye irritation category 1 according to Regulation (EC) No 1272/2008 (CLP) and Directive 67/548/EEC (DSD). However, general population is not intended to have eyes contact to the test material. Therefore, due to exposure based reasons no hazard for the general population is identified.
