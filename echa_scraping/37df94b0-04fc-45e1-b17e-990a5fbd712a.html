    Additional information - workers

    The typical pattern of worker and consumer exposure to 3-methoxy-3-methyl-butan-1-ol is low levels of exposure on a repeated basis. Short-term high exposures are considered unlikely given the high levels of control in place at sites formulating and using the substance. DNELs for long-term exposure are therefore adequate to protect against short-term exposures and no separate short-term DNELs are calculated.

    The oral subchronic toxicity NOAEL of 250 mg/kg bw/day is selected as critical endpoint on the basis of haematological findings and clinical biochemistry and is used as point of departure for the derivation of dermal DNELs. This endpoint is considered the most sensitive one and representative for adverse effects observed in different species and different studies available.

    The inhalation subchronic study is used as point of departure for the derivation of inhalatory DNELs.
