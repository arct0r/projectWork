    Explanation for the modification of the dose descriptor starting point

    Kinetics (metabolism, distribution and excretion) are considered to be similar for oral and inhalation intake in the absence of evidence of the opposite. Conversion of an oral NOAEL into a corrected NOAEC: For workers (8h exposure/day), the corrected inhalatory NOAEC = oral NOAEL * 1/sRVrat * ABSoral-rat /ABSinhal-human * sRVhuman/wRV = 1000 * 1/0.38 m3/kg/8h * ABSoral-rat /ABSinhal-human * 6.7 m3 (8h)/10 m3 (8h) = 1000 * 1/0.38 m3/kg/8h * 0.5 * 6.7 m3 (8h)/10 m3 (8h) = 1000 /0.38 * 0.5* (6.7/10) = 881.6 mg/m3.

    With ABS: Absorption, sRV: Standard Respiratory Volume; wRV: Worker Respiratory Volume; ABSoral-rat /ABSinhal-human= 50/100= 0.5, assuming no differences in inhalation absorption between rats and humans.
