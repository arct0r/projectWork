    Additional information - workers

    The hazard assessment conclusions and DNEL derivation are based on the following data:

    The acute oral LD50 of 1,2-Propylendiamine (1,2-PDA) in rats was determined to be 1300 mg/kg bw. In acute inhalation studies in rats the LC50  was > 49000 mg/m3. The LD50 in acute dermal toxicity studies in rabbits was 430 mg/kg bw.

    1,2-PDA was corrosive to the skin and eye.

    No gene mutagenic activity of 1,2-PDA was detected in bacteria and mammalian cells and no clastogenic or aneugenic effects were recorded in a chromosome aberration study with mammalian cells.

    In a short-term repeated dose study inhalation exposure of rats to 1,2-PDA did not lead to any exposure-related systemic toxicity. Local effect in the nasal cavity comprised acute inflammation, degeneration, atrophy, erosion/ulceration, necrosis of the epithelium (olfactory/respiratory epithelium) and suppurative exudate in the lumen. Under the current test conditions, the NOAEC for systemic toxicity was the highest tested concentration of 100 mg/m3, the NOAEC for local effect in nasal cavity was 5.0 mg/m3.

    In a developmental toxicity study the oral administration of a neutralized 1,2-PDA solution to pregnant rats led to systemic, maternal toxicity at 500 mg/kg bw/d, but no effects on gestational parameters or fetusus were observed at any dose level. So, the NOAEL for maternal toxicity was at 150 mg/kg bw/d and the NOAEL for prenatal developmental toxicity was at 500 mg/kg bw/d (highest dose tested).

    Based on the available data 1,2-PDA is classified as followed: Acute Tox. 4 (H302), Acute Tox. 3 (H311), Skin Corr. 1A (H314), Eye Damage 1 (H318). Additionally, 1,2-PDA is classified as Flam. Liquid 3 (H226).

     

     

    DNEL derivation:

    DNEL derivation for the test item is performed under consideration of the recommendations of ECHA, Guidance on information requirements and chemical safety assessment, Chapter R.8: Characterization of dose-response for human health (Version: 2.1, November 2012).

     

    Inhalation - Local effects - Long-term (workers)

    DNEL (based on the 28 days inhalation study in rats)

    Using a conservative approach, a worker DNEL for local effects (long-term inhalation exposure) is calculated. This worker long-term DNEL is considered to ensure an appropriate level of protection with regard to acute inhalation exposure (no high peaks of exposure expected).

    A 28-day inhalation study, conducted according to OECD TG 412 under GLP, was available for DNEL derivation. In this study Hannover Wistar rats were exposed to 5, 18.5 and 100 mg/m3 1,2-PDA for 28 days. No signs of systemic toxicity were observed at the highest concentration level of 100 mg/m3, while a NOAEC of 5 mg/m3 was established for local effects.
