    Explanation for the modification of the dose descriptor starting point

    The calculation of the DNEL is based on an oral NOAEL obtained from an OECD 421 study conducted with the registered substance (Wang, 2018).

    To take into account the interspecies difference between the rat and human the no observed adverse effect level (NOAEL) has to be corrected as follows according to ECHA guidance R.8 (2012):

    Corrected starting point for the inhalative route for workers:

    = NOAEL(oral) * (1/0.38 mÂ³/kg bw/day) * (ABSoral-rat/ABSinh-human) * 6.7 mÂ³ (8h) /10 mÂ³ (8h) * (days of exposure per week in rats / days of exposure per week worker)

    = 100 mg/kg bw/day * (1/0.38 mÂ³/kg bw/day) * (1/1) * 0.67 mÂ³ * (7/5) = 246.8 mg/mÂ³.

    Therefore, the corrected starting point for workers was 246.8 mg/mÂ³ for inhalation.

    (ABSoral-rat = oral absorption in rats, ABSinh-human = inhalation absorption rate in humans). In contrast to the recommendations of the ECHA Guidance, a factor of 1 (equal absorption of 100% assumed for the oral and the inhalative route for animals and humans) was included for the extrapolation from oral to inhalation absorption based on the following reason. As described in the toxicokinetic assessment an absorption via inhalation is quite unlikly and highest absorption rate is considered to be via oral route. Thus, an equal absorption is assumed when extrapolating from oral to inhalation route.
