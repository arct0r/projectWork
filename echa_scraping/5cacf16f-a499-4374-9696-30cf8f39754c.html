    Workers - Hazard for the eyes

    Local effects

    Hazard assessment conclusion
    no hazard identified
    Explanation for hazard conclusion
    [Not publishable]

    Additional information - workers

    The NOAELs for repeated dose toxicity, obtained in well performed and guideline congruent studies with oral diphenyl carbonate exposure of rats over a period of 11 to 18 weeks, were determined to be about 132 mg/kg bw/day for male and about 50 mg/kg bw/day for female rats. For the DNELs systemic derivation the lowest NOAEL of 50 mg/kg bw/day has been chosen as the more precautionary and appropriate starting point.

    Although according to the exposure scenarios for diphenyl carbonate peak exposures via inhalation, dermal or oral route can be excluded, the DNELs for acute systemic effects have been set as 3x the long-term systemic DNELs.

    Since diphenyl carbonate is not irritating to skin and eyes of rabbits, and has no sesnsitising properties, no DNEL for local effects is considered necessary.
