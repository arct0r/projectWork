    Explanation for the modification of the dose descriptor starting point

    Standard respiratory volume, human (sRVhuman) for 8 h per person (70 kg): 6.7 m3

    Standard respiratory volume of the rat (sRVrat) for 8 hours: 0.38 m3/kg bw

    Worker respiratory volume (wRV) for 8 hours with light physical activity per person: 10 m3

    Oral absorption of the rat/ inhalation absorption of humans (ABS dermal-rat / ABS inh-human): 1/2

    Correction for difference between human and experimental exposure conditions: 7 d rat/5 d worker

     

    Corrected NOAEC (inhalation) for workers:

    NOECcorr = NOAELdermal * (1/0.38 m³/kg bw/day) x (6.7 m³/10m³) * (7 days/5 days) * (ABSoral/ABSinh)

    NOAEC = 700 mg/kg bw/day * (1/0.38 m3/kg bw/day) * (6.7 m3 / 10 m3) * (7 days/5 days) * 1/2 = 863.95 mg/m³

    Additional information - workers

    Systemic long-term DNEL for inhalation exposure

    The respective NOAEC is based on a NOAEL of 700 mg/kg bw/day from a subchronic dermal rat study with the analogue substance DL-hexane-1,2-diol (CAS 6920-22-5) and considering a dermal bioavailability of 100%. It was modified using a human standard respiratory volume (sRVhuman) of 6.7 m3 for 8 hours per person (70 kg), a rat standard respiratory volume (sRVrat) of 0.38 m3/kg bw for 8 hours, a worker respiratory volume (wRV) of 10 m³ for 8 hours with light physical activity, the default quotient of ½ for dermal absorption of the rat and inhalation absorption of humans (ABS dermal-rat / ABS inh-human) and a correction for difference between human and experimental exposure conditions (7 days exposure of the rats/5 days exposure of worker per week) to 863.95 mg/m3 using the equation provided in the Guidance Document on Information Requirement, Chapter R8:

    NOECcorr = NOAELoral x 1/0.38 m³/kg bw/day x 6.7 m³/10m³ x 7d/5d x ABSoral/ABSinh

    Using assessment factors of (i) 2 for duration of exposure, (ii) 2.5 for remaining species differences and (iii) 5 for intraspecies extrapolation, a DNEL of 34.56 mg/m3 for long-term, systemic inhalative exposure was calculated.

     

    Systemic acute DNEL for inhalation exposure

    No acute inhalation toxicity study is available. According to ECHA Guidance on information requirements and chemical safety, Chapter R.8, Appendix R. 8-8, " a DNEL for acute toxicity should be derived if an acute toxicity hazard (leading to C&L) has been identified". The analogue substance DL-hexane-1,2-diol (CAS 6920-22-5) has low acute oral toxicity with the LD50 of 6166 mg/kg. Also for the inhalation route, no systemic toxic effects have to be expected after a single acute exposure. Therefore, the derivation of a DNEL is not required. Long-term DNELs are considered sufficient to ensure that acute effects do not occur.

     

    Local long-term and acute DNELs for inhalation exposure

    In the 90-day dermal study with the analogue substance DL-hexane-1,2-diol (CAS 6920-22-5), slight skin irritation was observed throughout the study at 700 and 1000 mg/kg/day, while at 350 mg/kg/day very slight irritation was only observed transiently, but not at the end of the study. The substance is furthermore classified for eye damage (category 1) and skin irritation (category 2) according to Regulation (EC) No 1272/2008 (CLP). Therefore, the substance might have the potential to cause some local respiratory effects at high concentrations. Thus, a qualitative risk assessment is conducted. Appropriate qualitative risk managements measures should be implemented to avoid exposure. The substance is assigned to the low hazard band in accordance with ECHA Guidance on information requirements and chemical safety assessment Part E: Risk Characterisation (2016).

     

    Systemic long-term DNEL for dermal exposure

    The NOAEL of 700 mg/kg bw/day from a subchronic dermal toxicity study according to OECD guideline 411 is used as POD. It was modified using a correction for difference between human and experimental exposure conditions (7 days exposure of the rats/5 days exposure of worker per week) to 980 mg/kg bw/day using the equation provided in the Guidance Document on Information Requirement, Chapter R8:

    NOAELcorr = NOAELdermal x 7d/5d

    Using assessment factors of (i) 2 for duration of exposure, (ii) 4 for interspecies differences (allometric scaling), (iii) 2.5 for remaining interspecies differences and (iiii) 5 for intraspecies extrapolation, a DNEL of 9.8 mg/kg bw/day for long-term, systemic dermal exposure was calculated.

     

    Systemic acute DNEL for dermal exposure

    No acute dermal toxicity study is available. According to ECHA Guidance on information requirements and chemical safety, Chapter R.8, Appendix R. 8-8, " a DNEL for acute toxicity should be derived if an acute toxicity hazard (leading to C&L) has been identified". The analogue substance DL-hexane-1,2-diol (CAS 6920-22-5) has low acute oral toxicity with a LD50 of 6166 mg/kg. Also for the dermal route, no systemic toxic effects have to be expected after a single acute exposure. Therefore the derivation of a DNEL is not required. Furthermore, long-term DNELs are considered sufficient to ensure that acute effects do not occur.

     

    Local long-term and acute DNELs for dermal exposure

    In the 90-day dermal study with the analogue substance DL-hexane-1,2-diol (CAS 6920-22-5), slight skin irritation was observed throughout the study at 700 and 1000 mg/kg/day, while at 350 mg/kg/day very slight irritation was only observed transiently, but not at the end of the study. The substance is furthermore classified for eye damage (category 1) and skin irritation (category 2) according to Regulation (EC) No 1272/2008 (CLP). Therefore, the substance might have the potential to cause some local dermal effects at high concentrations. Thus, a qualitative risk assessment is conducted. Appropriate qualitative risk managements measures should be implemented to avoid exposure. The substance is assigned to the low hazard band in accordance with ECHA Guidance on information requirements and chemical safety assessment Part E: Risk Characterisation (2016).

     

    Hazard for the eyes

     According to CLP Regulation (EC 1272/2008), the test substance is classified as eye corrosive (category 1). Thus, a qualitative risk assessment is conducted. Appropriate qualitative risk managements measures should be implemented to avoid exposure. The substance is assigned to the medium hazard band in accordance with ECHA Guidance on information requirements and chemical safety assessment Part E: Risk Characterisation (2016).

    Explanation for the modification of the dose descriptor starting point

    Dermal absorption of the rat/ oral absorption of humans (ABS dermal-rat / ABS oral-human): 1/2 

    A factor of 1/2 is used as a worst-case assumption. The analogue substance DL-hexane-1,2-diol (CAS 6920-22-5) showed a similar bioavailability after oral and dermal administration, even at the highest tested dermal dose of 1000 mg/kg/day no relevant systemic toxic effects were observed in the dermal 90-day study, and information on structurally related 1,2-alkanediols indicate NOAEL values of 1000 or higher in oral studies.

    Corrected NOAEC (oral) for general population:

    NOECcorr = NOAELdermal x ABSderm/ABSoral

    NOECcorr = 700 mg/kg bw/day x 1/2

    NOECcorr = 350 mg/kg bw/day

    Additional information - General Population

    Systemic long-term DNEL for inhalation exposure

    The respective NOAEC is based on a NOAEL of 700 mg/kg bw/day from a subchronic dermal rat study with the analogue substance DL-hexane-1,2-diol (CAS 6920-22-5) and considering a dermal bioavailability of 100%. It was modified  using a rat standard respiratory volume (sRVrat) of 1.15 m3/kg bw/day for 24 hours and the default quotient of ½ for dermal absorption of the rat and inhalation absorption of humans (ABS dermal-rat / ABS inh-human) to 304.5 mg/m³ using the equation provided in the Guidance Document on Information Requirement, Chapter R8:

    NOECcorr = NOAELoral x 1/1.15 m³/kg bw/day x ABSoral/ABSinh

    Using assessment factors of (i) 2 for duration of exposure, (ii) 2.5 for remaining species differences and (iii) 10 for intraspecies extrapolation, a DNEL of 6.09 mg/m3 for long-term, systemic inhalative exposure was calculated.

     

    Systemic acute DNEL for inhalation exposure

    No acute inhalation toxicity study is available. According to ECHA Guidance on information requirements and chemical safety, Chapter R.8, Appendix R. 8-8, " a DNEL for acute toxicity should be derived if an acute toxicity hazard (leading to C&L) has been identified". The analogue substance DL-hexane-1,2-diol (CAS 6920-22-5) has low acute oral toxicity with the LD50 of 6166 mg/kg. Also for the inhalation route not systemic toxic effect has to be expected after a single acute exposure. Therefore, the derivation of a DNEL is not required. Long-term DNELs are considered sufficient to ensure that acute effects do not occur.

     

    Local long-term and acute DNELs for inhalation exposure

    In the 90-day dermal study with the analogue substance DL-hexane-1,2-diol (CAS 6920-22-5, slight skin irritation was observed throughout the study at 700 and 1000 mg/kg/day, while at 350 mg/kg/day very slight irritation was only observed transiently, but not at the end of the study. The substance is furthermore classified for eye damage (category 1) and skin irritation (category 2) according to Regulation (EC) No 1272/2008 (CLP). Therefore, the substance might have the potential to cause some local respiratory effects at high concentrations. Thus, a qualitative risk assessment is conducted. Appropriate qualitative risk managements measures should be implemented to avoid exposure. The substance is assigned to the low hazard band in accordance with ECHA Guidance on information requirements and chemical safety assessment Part E: Risk Characterisation (2016).

     

    Systemic long-term DNEL for dermal exposure

    The NOAEL of 700 mg/kg bw/day from a subchronic dermal toxicity study according to OECD guideline 411 is used as POD. It did not have to be modified according to Guidance Document on Information Requirement, Chapter R8.

    Using assessment factors of (i) 2 for duration of exposure, (ii) 4 for interspecies differences (allometric scaling), (iii) 2.5 for remaining interspecies differences and (iiii) 10 for intraspecies extrapolation, a DNEL of 3.5 mg/kg bw/day for long-term, systemic dermal exposure was calculated.
