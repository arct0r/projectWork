    Hazard assessment conclusion
    low hazard (no threshold derived)
    Explanation for hazard conclusion
    [Not publishable]

    Additional information - General Population

    The starting point and predicted NOAEL for the oral DNEL derivation is a NOAEL of 315 mg/kgbw/day. Extrapolation of this to the inhalation route is not appropriate because a derived equivalent for the this route would be many times higher than the saturated vapour concentration at room temperature of this low volatility solvent. The skin absorption is low (relatively large molecule) and a NOAEL by the dermal route is likely to exceed the limit dose of 1000 mg/kgbw/day. (Based an estimated permeabilty of 0.035 mg/cm3/hr, assuming a 25 cm2 skin area exposed in an animal (rat), the maximum amount absorbed over 24 hours would be ~25 mg (or <100 mg/kgbw/day). Therefore extrapolation of the oral data to the dermal route is not appropriate either.
