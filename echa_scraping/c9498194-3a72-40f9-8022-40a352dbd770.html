    Explanation for the modification of the dose descriptor starting point

    An inhalative long-term study is not available. Therefore, the worker-DNEL long-term for inhalation route - systemic is derived from the oral NOAEL of 529 mg/kg bw/day, obtained in a Repeted dose Toxicity Study (OECD TG 453) in Wistar rats. The NOAECcorr. is calculated as follows:

    - standard respiratory volume rat = 0.38 m³/kg bw

    - standard respiratory volume human = 6.7 m³/8h

    - worker respiratory volume = 10 m³/8h

    - absorption (oral, rat) = 50 % (default)

    - absorption (inhalative, human) = 100 % (default)

    - experimental exposure time = 7 days/week

    - exposure time worker = 5 days/week

    --> modified dose descriptor (corrected inhalatory NOAEC) = 529 mg/kg bw/day * (1/0.38 m³/kg/d) * (6.7 m³ (8h)/10 m³ (8h)) * (50%/100%) * (7 exposure days/week; rat/5 exposure days/week; worker) = 653 mg/m³
