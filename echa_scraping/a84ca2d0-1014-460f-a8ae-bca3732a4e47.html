    AF for dose response relationship
    1
    Justification

    Default value. A concentration-dependent response was observed.

    AF for differences in duration of exposure
    6
    Justification

    Default value. Local effects were observed in repeated-dose toxicity studies (14-day and 28-day)

    AF for interspecies differences (allometric scaling)
    1
    Justification

    Default value. The exposure route is inhalation.

    AF for other interspecies differences
    2.5
    Justification

    Findings consisted in alterations in the nasal cavity. A greater sensitivity in rats is usually observed compared to humans because of the physiological differences in the rat nasal cavity (in particular due to the larger epithelium surface in contact with incoming airflow). Furthermore, a difference of sensitivity was observed between two rat strains. There were no findings in the nasal cavity of Wistar rats treated similarly. However, in the absence of information related to the mechanism leading to the nasal effects in Sprague-Dawley rats and whether metabolism is involved, a default assessment factor for residual interspecies differences is applied conservatively.

    AF for intraspecies differences
    5
    Justification

    Default value for workers

    AF for the quality of the whole database
    1
    Justification

    Default value. Effects on the nasal cavity were documented in a dose-range finding study (14-day) and a reliable 28-day study in Sprague-Dawley rats, not in Wistar rats treated for 28 days (males) and up to 47 days (females). Therefore the point of departure is considered conservative.

    AF for remaining uncertainties
    1
    Justification

    Default value.
