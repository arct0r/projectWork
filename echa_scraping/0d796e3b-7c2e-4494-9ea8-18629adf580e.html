    Workers - Hazard for the eyes

    Local effects

    Hazard assessment conclusion
    low hazard (no threshold derived)
    Explanation for hazard conclusion
    [Not publishable]

    Additional information - workers

    Oral uptake of reasonable amounts of sodium carbonate will result in a neutralisation in the stomach due to the presence of gastric acid. Sodium carbonate is not classified for acute oral toxicity, due to its relatively low oral toxicity (LD50 = 2800 mg/kg, rat). Therefore, it is not considered necessary to derive a DNELlocal for oral exposure to sodium carbonate
