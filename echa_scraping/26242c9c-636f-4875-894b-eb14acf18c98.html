    Additional information - workers

    Long-term exposure

    Sodium thioglycolate (NaTG) was choosen for long-term animal studies to act as an appropriate surrogate for thioglycolic acid and its salts. Correcting the NOAEL for molecular weight is regarded as not necessary, as it has a negligible effect on this value.

    Dermal, systemic effects

    The available data suggests no relevant differences in dermal absorption among salts of thioglycolic acid.

    In 90-day studies performed in rats and mice with sodium thioglycolate, the dermal NOAELs for systemic toxicity were higher than 180 and 360 mg/kg bw/day, respectively. The NOAEL > 360 mg/kg bw/day in mice is selected as a relevant dose-descriptor.

    Additional information - General Population

    DNELs for the general population have not been proposed because thioglycolates are generally used as an ingredient in cosmetic products. According to Article 14, paragraph 5(b) of of Regulation (EC) No.1907/2006, the chemical safety report does not need to include considerations of the risk to human health from end uses in cosmetic products within the scope of Directive 76/768/EEC.
