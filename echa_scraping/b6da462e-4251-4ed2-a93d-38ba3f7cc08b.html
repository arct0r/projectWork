    Additional information - workers

    General discussion

    As described in Section 1.2, the registration substance, Tall Oil Soap (TOS) is a complex UVCB substance containing a range of constituent types. The constituents of TOS are all naturally occurring secondary metabolites of pine trees and many other types of plant. Fatty acid constituents in particular are an important dietary source for humans, and the sterol constituent Î²-sitosterol is used as a supplement in certain types of fat spreads due to its cholesterol-lowering properties.

    The principal constituents are sodium salts of C14-C20 fatty acids and sodium salts of rosin acids. In addition, there are numerous minor constituents. A more detailed composition, with representative chemical structures, is shown in Table 5.0.1. Additional information can be found in a supporting report (PFA 2019).

    Where available, key endpoint data are reported for âwhole substanceâ and these are also used for classification conclusions for the registered substance. It is also important to consider the possible impact of individual constituents on the overall toxicity of the substance. Information on constituents has therefore been gathered from published sources and where possible original study reports have also been obtained and summarised.

    For endpoints where measured data for whole substance are not available, a weight of evidence approach has been followed based on constituent data. Constituent data for all endpoints are relevant to support read-across for the weight of evidence approach.

    In addition, some measured data are available for the related substances âDistilled Tall Oilâ, âCrude Tall Oilâ (CTO) and Tall Oil Rosin (TOR), which contain similar groups or blocks of constituents as the registered substance.

    For the purposes of environmental fate and exposure assessment, a âblockâ approach is used, whereby constituents with similar physicochemical and environmental properties are grouped together. Based on the available toxicological data, only Blocks 2 and 9 are considered to have effects that are relevant for assessment of humans exposed via the environment. Long-term systemic oral DNEL values have therefore been determined for these blocks; no relevant hazards are identified for the other blocks. The inhalation route is not considered relevant for humans exposed via the environment due to the very low volatility of the constituents.
