    Additional information - workers

    The most relevant routes of exposure for workers are the dermal and inhalation routes.

    Based on the available acute toxicity data magnesium hydroxide is not acutely toxic and therefore the derivation of a DNELlong-term will be sufficient to control potential risks associated with short-term exposures. In addition, based on the available data magnesium hydroxide is not irritating to skin and eyes and is not a skin sensitiser. It is also unlikely to be a respiratory irritant or sensitiser. Magnesium hydroxide based on the available data does not appear to elicit local toxicity effects.

    Additional information - General Population

    Based on the available acute toxicity data magnesium hydroxide is not acutely toxic and therefore the derivation of a DNEL long-term will be sufficient to control potential risks associated with short-term exposures.
