    Additional information - workers

    The short-term dermal DNEL for systemic effects is based on a 21-day dermal study in rabbits with ziram (corrected systemic NOAEL = 208 mg KDDC/kg bw/day).

    The short-term inhalation DNEL for systemic effects is based on the 90 -day oral study in rats with SDDC (NOAEL = 24.4 mg/kg bw/day). The starting point was corrected using the 10 mÂ³ breathing volume per 8-h shift with light physical activity.

    No quantitative dose-response data are available for local effects on skin and respiratory tract.

     

    The long-term dermal DNEL for systemic effects is based on the one-year feeding study in dogs (corrected NOAEL = 3.6 mg SDDC/kg bw/day). The NOAEL was corrected with the assumed dermal absorption value of 0.1%, whereas oral absorption is 60% (in rats).

    The long-term inhalation DNEL for systemic effects is based on the one-year feeding study in dogs (corrected NOAEL = 3.33 mg KDDC/kg bw/day).

    The starting point was corrected using an assumed breathing volume of 0.134 mÂ³/kg bw/8 h for a 15-kg dog. A 10 mÂ³ breathing volume per 8-h shift with light physical activity is assumed for the worker.
