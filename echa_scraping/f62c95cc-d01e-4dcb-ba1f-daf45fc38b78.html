    Explanation for the modification of the dose descriptor starting point

    An inhalative long-term study according to OECD TG 422 is available. However, this study is deemed inadequate for the derivation of the long term inhalative DNEL for workers, since no systemic effects were observed in this study and putting the NOEAC on a level with the high dose might be a poor estimate. Therefore, the worker-DNEL long-term for inhalation route - systemic is derived from the oral NOAEL of 340 mg/kg bw/day, obtained in the Extended One-Generation Reproductive Toxicity Study (OECD TG 443) in Wistar rats. The NOAECcorr. is calculated as follows:

    - standard respiratory volume rat = 0.38 m³/kg/8h

    - standard respiratory volume human = 6.7 m³/8h

    - worker respiratory volume = 10 m³/8h

    - absorption (oral, rat) = 50 % (default)

    - absorption (inhalative, human) = 100 % (default)

    - experimental exposure time = 7 days/week

    - exposure time worker = 5 days/week

    --> modified dose descriptor (corrected inhalatory NOAEC) = 340 mg/kg bw/day * (1/0.38 m³/kg/d) * (6.7 m³ (8h)/10 m³ (8h)) * (50%/100%) * (7 exposure days/week; rat/5 exposure days/week; worker) = 420 mg/m³
