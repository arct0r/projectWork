    Explanation for the modification of the dose descriptor starting point

    The ECHA guidance (Chapter R.8 of the Guidance on Information Requirements and Chemical Safety Assessment) indicates that acute DNELs do not need to be calculated for substances that are not classified as an acute toxicity hazard and where there is potential of high peak exposures (< 15 mins) that exceed the long term DNEL. Although DMBC is classified as an acute oral toxicity hazard (Category 4), no potential for high peak exposures exist, so no acute DNEL needs to be calculated.
