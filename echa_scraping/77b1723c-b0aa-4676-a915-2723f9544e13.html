    Explanation for the modification of the dose descriptor starting point

    Kinetics (metabolism, distribution and excretion) are considered to be similar for oral and inhalation intake in the absence of evidence of the opposite.

    Starting point: NOAEL of 430 mg/kg bw/day in a chronic toxicity study in rat.

    Conversion of an oral NOAEL into a corrected NOAEC:For workers (8h exposure/day), the corrected inhalatory NOAEC = oral NOAEL * 1/sRVrat * ABSoral-rat /ABSinhal-human * sRVhuman/wRV

    = 430 mg/kg bw/day * 1/0.38 m3/kg/8h * ABSoral-rat /ABSinhal-human * 6.7 m3(8h)/10 m3(8h)

    = 430 mg/kg bw/day * 1/0.38 m3/kg/8h * 50/100 * 6.7 m3(8h)/10 m3(8h)

    = 430 mg/kg bw/day/0.38 * Â½ * (6.7/10) = 380 mg/m3.

    With ABS: Absorption, sRV: Standard Respiratory Volume; wRV: Worker Respiratory Volume;

    ABSoral-rat /ABSinhal-human= 50/100= 1/2, assuming no differences in inhalation absorption between rats and humans.
